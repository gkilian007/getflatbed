## Why Nobody Talks About It (And Why That's Good for You)

Turkish Miles&Smiles is one of the best-kept secrets in the miles and points world — not because it's hard to use, but because it's overshadowed by more famous programs. Most European travelers default to Flying Blue, Miles & More, or their home carrier's program.

That leaves Turkish M&S with something incredibly valuable: **partner availability that other programs can't access, at prices that make every other program look overpriced.**

Here's the fundamental truth: when you book a Star Alliance partner flight through Turkish Miles&Smiles, you pay the lowest redemption rates in the Star Alliance ecosystem. Every time.

## The Istanbul Stopover Trick

Here's where it gets even better. Turkish Airlines has one of the most generous stopover policies of any airline: **you can include a free stopover in Istanbul (IST)** on most award tickets without paying extra miles.

Istanbul is a genuinely extraordinary city — spanning two continents, with world-class food, architecture, and culture. And Turkish Airlines's home terminal, the Istanbul Airport (IST), is one of the most stunning airports in the world.

A practical example: Flying from Madrid to Bangkok? Route it through Istanbul with a 2-night stopover on the outbound, then direct back. Same miles, two destinations.

Turkish Airlines also offers **"Turkish Stopover"** — a program where long-connection passengers get a free hotel night in Istanbul. Even if you're using someone else's award miles to fly through Istanbul, this can apply.

## How to Earn Miles&Smiles Miles

This is Turkish M&S's biggest weakness: earning is harder than with programs that have co-branded Spanish credit cards.

### Flying Turkish Airlines

The most direct way. Turkish Airlines operates daily flights from Madrid (MAD) and Barcelona (BCN) to Istanbul, with good connections onwards. Flying in business class earns miles at 150% of base miles, making premium cabin travel self-reinforcing.

### Hotel Transfers

**Marriott Bonvoy** transfers to Miles&Smiles at 60,000 Marriott = 25,000 Turkish miles (a 60:25 ratio, slightly below typical). If you stay at Marriott properties for business travel, this is a viable accumulation strategy.

**Hilton Honors** transfers at a poor ratio — not recommended.

### Miles&Smiles Credit Card in Turkey

If you have Turkish banking connections, the Turkish Miles&Smiles credit cards earn aggressively. This isn't practical for most Spanish residents, but worth knowing if you have business interests in Turkey.

### Transfer from Amex and Chase

**American Express Membership Rewards** does NOT directly transfer to Miles&Smiles — this is the program's main limitation for European point collectors.

**The workaround:** Transfer Amex MR to British Airways Avios, then use Avios to book oneworld routes instead of Turkish M&S for Star Alliance routes. They serve different ecosystems.

### The Earning Reality Check

Turkish M&S is primarily valuable as a **redemption account** rather than an earning account. The strategy for most Spanish travelers:
1. Earn miles in programs with better earning (Amex MR → Flying Blue, Iberia Avios)
2. Keep a Turkish M&S account for specific high-value redemptions
3. Transfer miles from hotel programs when you need a top-up

## Step-by-Step Booking Walkthrough

### Step 1: Create Your Account

Go to turkishairlines.com and register for Miles&Smiles. It's free. Use your real name exactly as it appears on your passport — this matters for award tickets.

### Step 2: Search for Award Availability

Go to the Miles&Smiles award booking portal. Select "Book Award Ticket" and search for your desired route.

**Pro tip:** Search point-to-point rather than city-to-city when possible. "MAD → JFK" rather than "Madrid → New York" gives you more control over which specific flights you see.

### Step 3: Check Partner Availability

If you don't see award space directly, the M&S system also searches Star Alliance partners. Make sure "Star Alliance Partners" is selected in your search.

**Alternative:** Check partner availability on the partner airline's own website first (e.g., check United's business class availability on united.com), then call Turkish Miles&Smiles to book the specific flights you've identified.

### Step 4: Book by Phone If Necessary

Turkish M&S's online booking portal for partner awards can be temperamental. If you find availability but can't book online, call their customer service line. They're generally helpful and can book partner award tickets over the phone.

Turkish Miles&Smiles customer service: available in English, 24/7.

### Step 5: Pay Taxes and Fees

Unlike some programs, Turkish M&S charges relatively low fuel surcharges on partner awards. Taxes for a transatlantic business class ticket are typically €50–€120, not the €500+ that some programs charge.

## Availability Tips

### When to Search

Partner award space availability varies wildly. Best windows for finding business class awards:

- **11+ months in advance** — Airlines release initial award inventory shortly after opening reservations
- **2–3 weeks before departure** — Last-minute unsold business class seats sometimes open up
- **Mid-week** — Tuesday and Wednesday often have better availability than weekends (fewer leisure travelers)

### Which Routes Have Best Availability

- **United Polaris** on MAD/BCN → US routes has good availability (United releases significant Star Alliance partner space)
- **ANA business class** to Japan is harder — book as far in advance as possible
- **Lufthansa to Germany/Europe** — excellent availability, good for positioning flights

### The Availability Calendar

Miles&Smiles doesn't have a visual calendar like some programs. Use ExpertFlyer or Seats.Aero to check partner award availability in a calendar view, then call M&S to book.
