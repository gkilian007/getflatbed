What if you could book business class for two people, but only pay miles for one?

That's exactly what companion certificates — also called companion vouchers — do. They're one of the most underutilized benefits in the premium travel world, and for couples or travel partners who fly together, they're genuinely transformative.

Here's everything you need to know.

## What Is a Companion Certificate?

A companion certificate is a voucher, usually earned through credit card spending or airline elite status, that allows you to book a second passenger on the same flight at a drastically reduced cost — often just taxes and fees.

In practice: You book business class from Madrid to São Paulo for 85,000 Avios plus €400 in taxes. With a companion certificate, you add your travel partner for just €400 more — no additional Avios required. Two people, business class, for the Avios cost of one.

The value is enormous. On a long-haul route where redemptions cost 85,000+ Avios per person, a companion voucher is worth 85,000 Avios — potentially over €1,500 in value.

## Airlines and Programs That Offer Companion Certificates

### British Airways — The 2-4-1 Companion Voucher

**How to earn:** Spend £10,000 on the British Airways American Express Premium Plus card in a calendar year (UK market). The companion voucher earns after crossing the spend threshold.

**What it gives you:** One companion flies for just the taxes and fees on any Avios redemption — including business class long haul. Valid for two years from earning date.

**Routes from Spain:** BA flies from Madrid (MAD) and Barcelona (BCN) to London Heathrow (LHR), with excellent onward connections to North America, Asia, and Africa. The voucher works on the full journey if booked as a single redemption.

**The math:** MAD → JFK via LHR in business class costs approximately 90,000 Avios per person. With the companion voucher: 90,000 Avios for two people + 2x taxes (~€450 each). Total: 90,000 Avios + ~€900 for two business class seats to New York.

**Rating:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ — One of the best companion voucher programs globally

### Iberia Plus — The Companion Voucher

**How to earn:** Iberia offers companion vouchers through the Iberia Visa premium credit card (specific spend threshold) and sometimes as promotions for Iberia Plus Platinum members.

**What it gives you:** A companion flies at a reduced Avios cost or for just taxes on select Iberia routes.

**Routes from Spain:** Iberia's hub is Madrid (MAD), with nonstop long-haul routes to New York, Los Angeles, Miami, Chicago, São Paulo, Buenos Aires, Mexico City, and more.

**Best use cases:**
- MAD → JFK: 68,000 Avios × 2 normally; companion voucher reduces this to ~68,000 total + 2x taxes
- MAD → GRU (São Paulo): 85,000 Avios × 2 normally; companion voucher for similar savings

**Rating:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ — Excellent for Iberia loyalists, best value on South American routes

### American Airlines — AAdvantage Companion Certificate

**How to earn:** Available through certain AAdvantage co-branded credit card products, primarily in the US market. Less accessible for Spanish residents without US credit history.

**What it gives you:** A companion flies at a discounted award rate on select American Airlines routes.

**Routes from Spain:** AA flies MAD → JFK and MAD → PHL (Philadelphia) nonstop.

**For Spanish residents:** This is harder to access due to the US-centric credit card requirement, but worth knowing if you have US financial relationships.

**Rating:** ⭐⭐⭐ — Limited accessibility for Spain-based travelers

### Finnair — Plus Companion Award

**How to earn:** Available through Finnair Plus elite status and certain promotions.

**Routes relevant to Spain:** Finnair connects Barcelona to Helsinki (HEL) and offers excellent long-haul connections to Asia (Japan, South Korea, Singapore) via Helsinki.

**The Asian connection advantage:** Helsinki is actually one of the shortest routes to Asia from Europe (due to polar routing). If you're flying to Japan or Southeast Asia, Finnair's Business class + companion voucher via HEL can be an excellent option.

## Rules and Restrictions: What to Watch For

Companion certificates come with conditions. Before you make plans around one, understand the restrictions:

### Fare Class Restrictions

Most companion vouchers are valid only on **award bookings** (using miles), not on discounted cash fares. You can't use a companion voucher on a €800 business class sale fare and pay nothing for your companion.

Some programs specify that the ticket must be booked in a specific award fare class (often "I" or "O" class for business award). If award seats aren't available in those classes, the voucher can't be used.

### Blackout Dates

Holiday periods (Christmas, Easter, summer peak) often have blackout dates where companion vouchers are invalid. Plan around these — the voucher is most useful in low and shoulder seasons anyway.

### Same Itinerary Requirement

Both passengers must fly exactly the same flights, same dates, same cabin. You can't use one voucher for different routing or dates.

### Expiration

Companion vouchers typically expire 12–24 months from earning. Don't let it lapse — book any trip rather than let valuable miles go unused.

### Booking Windows

Some vouchers can only be used for bookings made X months in advance, or alternatively only within X months of travel. Read the specific terms.

## How to Combine Companion Vouchers with Other Benefits

The most sophisticated approach combines multiple benefits:

### Companion Voucher + Miles Promotion

Watch for Avios sale events or Flying Blue Promo Awards. If redemption costs drop 25%, your companion voucher gets even more valuable — you're using fewer miles AND bringing a companion free.

### Companion Voucher + Stopover

British Airways and Iberia allow stopovers on certain award bookings. Route MAD → (stopover in London/Madrid) → destination for no extra miles, then use the companion voucher. Two destinations for the price of one redemption.

### Companion Voucher + Lounge Access

Both passengers on a companion voucher booking get business class lounge access. One companion voucher effectively doubles your lounge access occasions — valuable if you travel regularly.

## Step-by-Step: Booking with a Companion Certificate

### Step 1: Check Voucher Terms

Log into your Iberia Plus or British Airways Executive Club account. Find your companion voucher under "My Benefits" or "My Vouchers." Note the expiration date and any restrictions.

### Step 2: Find Award Availability

Search for the award seats you want. For Iberia Avios: use iberia.com and search for "Avios" booking. For BA 2-4-1: use ba.com's "Avios" search. Make sure the fare class shown is one where your voucher applies.

**Pro tip:** Search for 2 passengers from the start. Award availability for 2 seats together is lower than for 1 seat — if you can only find 1 seat, the companion voucher can't be used.

### Step 3: Add the Voucher at Checkout

During the booking process, you'll be prompted to apply any vouchers. The companion voucher appears in your account and is automatically detected. If it doesn't appear, contact customer service before completing the booking.

### Step 4: Pay Taxes Twice

You'll pay the full taxes and fees for both passengers — this is expected. The savings are in the Avios (you use one person's worth instead of two).

### Step 5: Confirm Both Itineraries

After booking, check that both passenger itineraries show correctly in your account and that both passengers have seat assignments in the business cabin.
