Credit cards are one of the most powerful tools for earning business class miles without flying. In Spain, the market is smaller than the US or UK, but there are genuinely excellent options for savvy travelers. Here's the complete 2026 guide.

## Understanding Points Currencies

Before diving into specific cards, you need to understand the two types of rewards:

**Fixed airline miles** — Earn miles directly into one airline's program (e.g., Iberia Avios). Simple, but limits flexibility.

**Transferable points** — Earn points in a flexible currency (e.g., American Express Membership Rewards) that you can transfer to multiple airline programs. More complex but dramatically more valuable.

**The principle:** Transferable points are almost always better. They give you the flexibility to route to whichever airline has the best availability when you're ready to book.

## Card 1: American Express Platinum Spain

**Type:** Transferable points (Membership Rewards)
**Annual fee:** €700
**Welcome bonus:** Typically 80,000–100,000 MR points (check current offer)

The American Express Platinum is the best premium travel card available in the Spanish market. The annual fee is high, but the card pays for itself through travel benefits alone.

### Transfer Partners

American Express Membership Rewards in Spain transfers to:
- **Iberia Plus Avios** (1:1 ratio)
- **British Airways Executive Club Avios** (1:1 ratio)
- **Air France/KLM Flying Blue** (1:1 ratio)
- **Emirates Skywards** (1:1 ratio)
- **Etihad Guest** (1:1 ratio)

This flexibility is the card's superpower. You earn one currency and deploy it to whichever program offers the best value for your target route.

### Key Benefits for Business Class Travelers

- **Lounge access:** Priority Pass membership (access to 1,300+ airport lounges worldwide)
- **Travel insurance:** Comprehensive coverage including flight cancellation and delay
- **Hotel upgrades:** Amex Fine Hotels & Resorts program with automatic room upgrades and late checkout
- **Global Entry/TSA Pre✓ fee credit** (for US travelers)

### The Welcome Bonus Math

80,000 Membership Rewards points transferred to Iberia Plus = 80,000 Avios.
One-way Madrid → New York business class costs 68,000 Avios.

The welcome bonus alone nearly covers a transatlantic business class seat.

**Best for:** Frequent travelers who value flexibility, lounge access, and premium travel benefits

## Card 2: Iberia Visa

**Type:** Direct Avios earning
**Annual fee:** €0 (standard) / €135 (Iberia Plus Visa Premium)
**Welcome bonus:** 10,000–40,000 Avios (varies by offer)

The most straightforward option for Spanish travelers who primarily fly Iberia. The no-annual-fee version earns 1 Avios per euro spent; the premium version earns 1.5 Avios per euro.

### The Companion Voucher

The Iberia Plus Visa Premium's most valuable benefit is the **companion voucher**: spend €20,000 on the card in a calendar year and receive a voucher allowing a companion to fly business class for just taxes and fees on an Iberia long-haul award booking.

For a couple, this doubles the value of your Avios. One person's miles cover both seats — you just pay two sets of taxes (typically €200–€400 per person).

### Earning Rate Reality Check

At 1 Avios per euro, earning toward a transatlantic business class redemption (68,000 Avios) requires €68,000 in spending. At 1.5x, that drops to ~€45,000. This illustrates why the welcome bonus is so crucial — it provides a quantum jump in your balance.

**Best for:** Iberia loyalists, couples who can use the companion voucher

## Maximizing Welcome Bonuses: The Strategy

Welcome bonuses are where most serious miles earners actually get their points. Here's the optimized approach:

### Meeting Minimum Spend Requirements

Welcome bonuses usually require spending a minimum amount within 3 months (e.g., "spend €3,000 in the first 3 months"). Strategies to meet this naturally:

- Pay quarterly estimated taxes with the card
- Pay rent (if your landlord accepts card payments or via payment services)
- Use the card for all business expenses
- Pay annual subscriptions and insurance premiums all at once on the card

**Never manufacture spending** — the miles value rarely justifies the effort or cost.

### The Timing Play

Apply for a new card before a major planned purchase (home renovation, annual trip, business equipment) to make meeting the minimum spend effortless.

### Churning: Not Recommended in Spain

In the US, some travelers apply for multiple cards, earn bonuses, then cancel and reapply. In Spain, this is more limited — American Express specifically has rules about bonus eligibility if you've had the card before. The annual fee cards are also significant enough that the strategy requires careful calculation.
