Madrid to New York is one of the most competitive transatlantic routes in the world. That competition — plus Spain's position as a major oneworld hub — makes it one of the best routes in Europe to score business class at a fraction of the retail price.

Retail business class on MAD → JFK/EWR/BOS ranges from €2,800 to €5,500 round trip. But savvy travelers book the same seats for €290–€800, or redeem miles for the equivalent of €90–€200 per seat. Here's exactly how.

## The Airlines That Fly MAD to New York

### Direct Routes (No Stops)

**Iberia (MAD → JFK)** — Daily nonstop. Iberia's A350 features fully lie-flat seats in a 1-2-1 configuration, meaning every seat has direct aisle access. The food is outstanding by transatlantic standards. The IB flagship for this route.

**Iberia (MAD → BOS)** — Seasonal service to Boston Logan. Less competition means fares sometimes stay elevated, but also means fewer competitors for award space.

**American Airlines (MAD → JFK, PHL)** — Codeshare heavy with Iberia. AA's Flagship Business is solid; the 777-300ER on this route has a competitive product.

### One-Stop Routes

**British Airways (MAD → LHR → JFK/EWR/BOS)** — Connection at Heathrow. BA's Club World business class is older but still lie-flat; the Heathrow connection adds time but opens up more scheduling flexibility.

**Level/Iberia Express (MAD → Various)** — Low-cost options that don't offer business class as understood here. Not relevant for this guide.

**Air France/KLM (MAD → CDG/AMS → JFK)** — Connection via Paris or Amsterdam. Air France La Première/Business and KLM World Business Class are both excellent products. Good option if nonstop Iberia is unavailable.

**Lufthansa (MAD → FRA/MUC → JFK)** — Frankfurt or Munich connection. Lufthansa's business class is reliable. The A380 on FRA → JFK has one of the best business class products currently flying.

**Turkish Airlines (MAD → IST → JFK)** — Istanbul connection adds time but Turkish business class is excellent (direct aisle access seats, Istanbul stopover opportunity). Best for using Miles&Smiles.

### Finding Discounted Cash Fares

**The advance booking window:** For Iberia specifically, the best discounted business class fares appear **10–13 months** in advance (when the new schedule opens) or **3–6 weeks** before departure (unsold seats).

**Midweek departures:** Tuesday and Wednesday departures are consistently cheaper than Thursday–Sunday.

**The January/September windows:** Late January and September represent the lowest demand periods on transatlantic routes. This is when airlines release "Sale" fares that can bring business class down to €800–€1,500 round trip legitimately.

## Miles Redemptions: The Best Programs for MAD → JFK

### Option 1: Iberia Plus Avios (Best for Most People)

**Cost:** 68,000 Avios one-way (136,000 round trip, or use companion voucher)
**Airline:** Iberia nonstop MAD → JFK
**Taxes:** ~€180–€220 per person
**Effective price equivalent:** ~€0.04–0.06 per mile

How to earn 68,000 Avios:
- Amex Platinum welcome bonus (80,000 MR = 80,000 Avios)
- Iberia Visa spending
- Flying with Iberia/BA/AA

**Sweet spot:** This is the gold standard for this route. Direct booking, excellent product, reasonable surcharges.

### Option 2: Turkish Miles&Smiles (Best Raw Value)

**Cost:** 45,000 miles one-way
**Airline:** United Polaris or AA Flagship Business (via Star Alliance/oneworld)
**Taxes:** ~€80–€150
**Effective price equivalent:** ~€0.03 per mile

Turkish M&S is the cheapest miles option for this route in the Star Alliance ecosystem. If you have or can earn 45,000 Miles&Smiles miles, this is your best move.

**The catch:** Booking United or AA flights through Turkish M&S requires either online booking (can be buggy) or a phone call to M&S customer service.

### Option 3: Flying Blue (Good for Delta Connection)

**Cost:** 50,000–70,000 miles one-way (varies, watch for Promo Awards at 37,500)
**Airline:** Delta One business class (via KLM connection CDG → JFK)
**Taxes:** ~€150–€200
**Effective price equivalent:** Variable

Flying Blue Promo Awards sometimes discount MAD → JFK routes by 25–50%. If you catch a promo, 37,500 miles for Delta One business class is exceptional value.

## Aircraft and Seat Guide: MAD → JFK

### Iberia A350-900

Iberia's flagship aircraft for this route. Configuration: 1-2-1 (all direct aisle access).

**Best seats:**
- **Row 1, seats A or K** (window, extra legroom, no seat in front)
- **Row 1, seat D or G** (aisle middle seats, extra space ahead)
- **Rows 3–5, window seats** — excellent privacy

**Avoid:** Middle of the cabin, rows 7–9 where foot rests can feel cramped

### American Airlines Boeing 777-200

AA's 777 on this route has a 2-2-2 configuration — not all-aisle access. The window seats (A/K) have a privacy partition but require climbing over seatmates for middle seats.

**Best seats:**
- **Business Bulkhead (Row 6):** Window seats, extra space, no recline issue from seat in front
- **1A/1K:** Privacy, space, first to deplane

**Avoid:** Middle sections (D/G rows) unless traveling with a companion

### Lufthansa A380 (via FRA)

If routing via Frankfurt, Lufthansa's A380 upper deck has an exceptional business class. 2-2-2 configuration but the seats recline completely flat.

**Best seats:**
- **Upper deck, window seats** — incredibly private
- **Seat 11A/K** — bulkhead upper deck
