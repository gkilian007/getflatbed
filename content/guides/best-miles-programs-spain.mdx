Spain is one of the best-positioned countries in Europe for scoring business class on miles. Madrid-Barajas (MAD) and Barcelona-El Prat (BCN) are major hubs with connections to virtually every continent. The question isn't whether you can fly business class on points from Spain — it's which program gives you the most value per mile earned.

After analyzing redemption rates, partner networks, and real availability on routes from Spain, here are the five programs every Spanish traveler should know.

## 1. Iberia Plus Avios — The Local Champion

**Best for:** Oneworld partners, Madrid hub flights, transatlantic on Iberia

Iberia Plus is the natural starting point for any Spanish traveler building a miles strategy. As Spain's flag carrier and a founding member of the oneworld alliance, Iberia gives you access to a powerful global network through a program you can earn in from daily spending.

### Why It Works

Iberia Plus uses a distance-based pricing model for many redemptions, which means short-to-medium haul routes are excellent value. A return business class ticket from Madrid to London can cost as few as 34,000 Avios — less than many programs charge for a single one-way.

For long haul, the best sweet spots are:
- **MAD → JFK (New York):** 68,000 Avios one-way in business
- **MAD → GRU (São Paulo):** 85,000 Avios one-way in business
- **MAD → MEX (Mexico City):** 68,000 Avios one-way in business

### Earning Avios in Spain

- **Iberia Visa card** — The easiest way to earn directly
- **British Airways Executive Club** — Avios are shared between BA and Iberia
- **American Express Membership Rewards** — Transfer to Iberia at 1:1
- **Flying with Iberia, British Airways, American Airlines, and other oneworld partners**

### The Companion Voucher

Iberia Plus's secret weapon is the companion voucher available through certain credit card products — allowing you to bring a travel companion for just the taxes when you book a business class redemption. For couples, this effectively halves the cost of flying business.

**Rating:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐½ — Best for MAD-hub travelers with Iberia loyalty

## 2. Turkish Miles&Smiles — The Secret Weapon

**Best for:** Star Alliance partners, incredible long-haul value, Istanbul hub

If Iberia Plus is the obvious choice, Turkish Miles&Smiles is the insider secret that experienced points collectors have been quietly using for years. Miles&Smiles offers some of the lowest redemption rates in the entire Star Alliance ecosystem, with access to premium airlines like United, Lufthansa, ANA, and Singapore Airlines.

### Why It's Underrated

While European travelers often default to Lufthansa Miles & More or Flying Blue, Turkish Miles&Smiles charges significantly fewer miles for the same seats. Example comparisons for business class:

| Route | Miles & More | Flying Blue | Turkish M&S |
|-------|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| MAD → JFK | 110,000 | 70,000 | 45,000 |
| MAD → NRT | 150,000 | 100,000 | 75,000 |
| MAD → GRU | 120,000 | 90,000 | 65,000 |

### Earning Without Flying

Turkish Miles&Smiles has credit card partnerships through Miles&More in Spain and some co-branded products. You can also earn through Marriott Bonvoy hotel transfers (transfer 60,000 Marriott points to get 25,000 M&S miles).

**Rating:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ — Best raw value for long-haul, underused by Spanish travelers

## 3. Air France/KLM Flying Blue — The Transatlantic Master

**Best for:** North America routes, SkyTeam partners, Flash sales

Flying Blue, the combined loyalty program of Air France and KLM, is a favorite for transatlantic redemptions — particularly for routes connecting through Paris CDG or Amsterdam AMS, which are easy connections from Spain.

### Promo Awards: The Real Value

Flying Blue runs monthly "Promo Rewards" sales where select routes are discounted 25–50% in miles. These sales have included business class from Europe to North America for as few as 30,000 miles one-way. If you hold Flying Blue miles and watch for these promotions, you can get exceptional value.

### Star Alliance... Wait, No — SkyTeam

Flying Blue is a SkyTeam program, giving you access to Delta, Korean Air, China Eastern, and more. The Delta One business class product (lie-flat, excellent dining) can be booked via Flying Blue, often for fewer miles than Delta's own program would charge.

### Earning in Spain

Flying Blue doesn't have a dedicated Spanish credit card, but American Express Membership Rewards transfers to Flying Blue at 1:1, making the Amex Platinum Spain card an excellent earning vehicle.

**Rating:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ — Excellent for transatlantic, particularly with Promo Awards

## 4. Lufthansa Miles & More — The European Network

**Best for:** European connections, Lufthansa Group flights, Star Alliance

Miles & More is the largest frequent flyer program in Europe by membership, and it remains useful for Spanish travelers despite its reputation for high redemption costs. The program shines for intra-European premium travel and for accessing the full Star Alliance network.

### The Premium Economy Bridge

One underrated strategy with Miles & More: using miles to upgrade from economy to business class on Lufthansa flights, rather than booking full award seats. Upgrade awards can be significantly cheaper than outright business class awards.

### The Frankfurt/Munich Hub Advantage

Many long-haul routes from Spain connect through Frankfurt (FRA) or Munich (MUC). If you're already flying Lufthansa Group carriers for these connections, earning Miles & More makes sense for the lifetime value of those miles.

### The Problem: Partner Redemptions Are Expensive

Miles & More charges 130,000–150,000 miles for transatlantic business class with partners — significantly more than Turkish M&S or Flying Blue. For partner awards, this program rarely offers the best value.

**Rating:** ⭐⭐⭐ — Useful for Lufthansa Group loyalists, not ideal for partner awards

---

## 5. Emirates Skywards — The Premium Product Champion

**Best for:** A380 business class, Dubai hub, luxury long haul

Emirates Skywards deserves a spot on this list not for redemption efficiency, but for what you can book with it: **the Emirates A380 business class**, widely considered one of the best aviation experiences in the world. Mini-bar, onboard lounge, fully lie-flat seat — all in a cabin that makes other airlines' products feel cramped.

### The Dubai Gateway

Emirates flies daily from Madrid (MAD) and Barcelona (BCN) to Dubai, making it easy to connect onwards to Asia, Australia, and East Africa via the world's most capable airline network.

### Redemption Rates

Emirates Skywards charges are moderate — typically 65,000–85,000 miles one-way in business class to Asia from Europe. The challenge is earning enough Skywards miles, as Emirates has limited credit card partnerships in Spain.

### Marriott Bonvoy Transfer

Marriott Bonvoy transfers to Emirates Skywards at a 3:1 ratio (60,000 Marriott = 20,000 Skywards). If you stay at Marriott properties for work, this is your path to Emirates miles.

**Rating:** ⭐⭐⭐½ — Worth it specifically for the Emirates product, less efficient for other routes

## The Smart Strategy: Use Multiple Programs

The sophisticated miles traveler doesn't pick one program and earn everything there. Instead:

1. **Earn Iberia Avios** for your regular Iberia flying and day-to-day credit card spending
2. **Keep a Turkish M&S account** for when you need to book premium Star Alliance partners at a discount
3. **Watch Flying Blue Promo Awards** every month for transatlantic opportunities
4. **Transfer Amex MR or Marriott points** to whichever program has the best availability for your target trip

The key insight: **miles are currencies, and different currencies buy different things at different prices.** The best deal on a flight to Tokyo might be Turkish M&S miles this month and Iberia Avios next month.
